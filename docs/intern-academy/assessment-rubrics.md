# Assessment Rubrics

Three assessments span the 12-week programme. Each is scored on the same 5-criterion structure for consistency, but with different weights and focus areas.

**Scoring scale:** 1 = Below expectations · 2 = Approaching · 3 = Meeting · 4 = Exceeding · 5 = Outstanding

A passing score is **15/25** (60%). Below 12/25 triggers the improvement plan (see "Escalation").

---

## Month 1 Assessment — Consultancy Plan Pitch (Week 4)

**Context:** Each intern presents what they learned from the 100-ticket Customer Support Academy. The deliverable is a "consultancy plan" — what the company should change based on the data they collected.

**Format:** 10-minute presentation + 3 minutes Q&A in the Monday meeting.

| # | Criterion | What we look for |
|---|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | **Analytical depth** | Did they categorise tickets meaningfully? Are the top 5 issues backed by counts/examples? Did they look for root causes or just symptoms? |
| 2 | **Actionability** | Are the recommended fixes concrete and implementable? Do they specify *what* to change, *who* should do it, and *how* to verify? |
| 3 | **Business impact thinking** | Did they estimate the impact of fixes (time saved, tickets prevented, conversion lifted)? Do they distinguish high-impact from cosmetic changes? |
| 4 | **Presentation clarity** | Logical flow, clean visuals, hits time limit, handles Q&A confidently. |
| 5 | **Initiative & curiosity** | Evidence they went beyond the minimum: extra interviews, tool exploration, original framings, surprising findings. |

**Score thresholds:**
- 22–25 = Outstanding (consider for visibility / fast-track)
- 18–21 = Strong
- 15–17 = Met expectations
- 12–14 = Conditional pass — written feedback + Week 6 check-in
- <12 = Below expectations — trigger improvement plan

**Common pitfalls:**
- Listing issues without counting them
- "More training" as a fix (not actionable)
- No prioritisation between recommendations
- Reading from slides instead of presenting

## Month 2 Assessment — Big Target Pitch (Week 8)

**Context:** Each intern delivers a meaningful project within their chosen specialisation track, executed over 4 weeks.

**Format:** 10-minute presentation + 3 minutes Q&A in the Monday meeting.

| # | Criterion | What we look for |
|---|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | **Outcome delivered** | Did they ship something? Is it usable/measurable? Did they hit the success metric they defined? |
| 2 | **Methodology** | Was the approach sound? Did they iterate based on data? Did they pivot when needed and explain why? |
| 3 | **Independence** | How much hand-holding did they need? Did they unblock themselves or wait? Did they ask the right questions early? |
| 4 | **Reflection quality** | Honest about what didn't work. Specific learnings, not generic ones. Clear hypothesis on what they'd do differently. |
| 5 | **Communication** | Clean narrative, clear cause→effect, hits time limit, fields hard questions without defensiveness. |

**Score thresholds:**
- 22–25 = Outstanding (strong hire signal)
- 18–21 = Strong
- 15–17 = Met expectations
- 12–14 = Conditional pass — Month 3 with closer track-lead supervision
- <12 = Below expectations — exit conversation

**Common pitfalls:**
- "I researched X" without producing anything
- Defining the success metric retroactively
- Blaming external factors instead of owning the result
- Picking a too-large project and shipping nothing

---

## Graduation Project — Final Presentation (Week 12)

**Context:** Final presentation summarising the full internship — what they owned, what they built, what they learned, what's next.

**Format:** 15-minute presentation + 5 minutes Q&A in the final Monday meeting.

| # | Criterion | What we look for |
|---|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | **Ownership demonstrated** | Did they truly run a function? Can they show before/after metrics? Did they make decisions, not just execute? |
| 2 | **Body of work** | Cumulative output across 3 months. Quality and quantity. Portfolio-ready artefacts. |
| 3 | **Growth arc** | Visible difference between Month 1 and Month 3. Specific examples of skills built or mindset shifts. |
| 4 | **Future thinking** | What would they do if given another month? What did they leave half-finished and why? Where do they want to go next? |
| 5 | **Personal goals** | Did they hit the 3 personal goals they set in Week 1? If not, what got in the way? |

**Score thresholds:**
- 22–25 = Outstanding (top hire candidate, formal reference)
- 18–21 = Strong (recommendation letter, network access)
- 15–17 = Met expectations (positive reference, programme certificate)
- 12–14 = Marginal (reference on request only, no proactive recommendation)
- <12 = Did not pass (no reference, programme acknowledgment only)

**Common pitfalls:**
- Slide deck is a list of tasks, not a story
- No evidence of metric movement
- "I learned a lot" without specifics
- Avoiding the personal goals reflection

---

## Escalation — Below 12/25 (any assessment)

If an intern scores below 12/25 on the Month 1 or Month 2 assessment:

1. **Same day:** 1:1 conversation with Tom (programme lead) — frame as "we want you to succeed, here's what we saw"
2. **Within 48h:** Written 2-week improvement plan
   - 3 specific things to change
   - Daily 5-minute check-ins with buddy
   - Weekly 30-minute review with Tom
3. **End of 2 weeks:** Re-evaluation
   - Improved → continue programme normally
   - Not improved → respectful exit conversation, generic reference letter for participation

**The goal of escalation is recovery, not punishment. Most underperformance is fixable if caught early.**

## Scoring logistics

- Each panellist (Luis, Peter, Tom + relevant track lead) scores independently during the presentation
- Scores entered into Business OS academy view immediately after
- Final score = average across all panellists
- Written feedback provided within 24h
- Intern sees scores + feedback in their portal
