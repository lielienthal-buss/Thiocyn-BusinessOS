### 1. Nature of the Engagement

1.1 The Fellow joins the **House of Sustainable Brands (HSB) Fellowship Program** operated by Thiocyn GmbH.

1.2 This is an **unpaid, fixed-term, fully remote orientation-fellowship** of **12 weeks (less than 3 months)**, starting on **[YYYY-MM-DD]** and ending on **[YYYY-MM-DD]** (the *"Fellowship Period"*). **Extensions require a new agreement** and may trigger different legal treatment (e.g. German Mindestlohngesetz § 22 becomes applicable for voluntary internships exceeding 3 months).

1.3 **This Agreement does not constitute an employment relationship.** The Fellow is not a "Mitarbeiter" or "Arbeitnehmer" under German labor law, is not subject to direction-and-control typical of employment, and does not receive remuneration. Thiocyn is not the Fellow's employer, does not withhold social-security contributions, and does not provide health insurance.

1.4 The Fellow acknowledges that they are responsible for their own:
- Health insurance coverage (Thiocyn will request a brief self-declaration)
- Tax and social-security obligations in their country of residence
- Compliance with any local laws governing unpaid internships / educational fellowships

### 2. Scope and Activities

2.1 The Fellowship is structured in four phases — Onboarding, Foundation, Specialisation, and Ownership — across 12 weeks, with assessments at Weeks 4, 8, and 12. Details are in the HSB Intern Academy documentation.

2.2 The Fellow will work on learning assignments, a specialisation "Big Target," and a graduation project. Work products may include but are not limited to marketing campaigns, creative assets, operational analyses, code, or business documents.

2.3 **Time commitment:** The Fellow commits to **up to 30 hours per week** on Fellowship activities, **self-scheduled at the Fellow's sole discretion**. Thiocyn does not prescribe working hours, working location, or required online-presence times, except for pre-announced live sessions (primarily the weekly Monday Meeting via Zoom — approx. 60 minutes, CET). The Fellow independently determines when, where, and in which sequence they engage with assignments. This self-scheduling is a constitutive feature of the Fellowship and is intended to distinguish it from an employment relationship.

2.4 The Fellow's work is guided by the **Programme Lead** (currently Danylo Kutsiuk) and a **Buddy** (full-time HSB employee). Assignment of the Buddy happens in Week 1.

### 3. Location and Working Arrangements

3.1 The Fellowship is **fully remote and international**. The Fellow works from their country of residence and is not required to visit any HSB office.

3.2 Communication is primarily asynchronous via Slack and the Business OS portal. Live meetings happen in **Central European Time (CET / CEST)** — the Fellow is responsible for accommodating this in their schedule for the Monday Meeting.

3.3 The Fellow is not entitled to a company laptop, workspace, or other physical equipment. Equipment costs incurred during the Fellowship are the Fellow's own unless expressly pre-approved by the Programme Lead or Owner.

### 4. Confidentiality (NDA)

4.1 The Fellow will have access to confidential information about HSB and its portfolio brands (Thiocyn, Dr. Severin, Take A Shot, Paigh, Timber & John, Wristr), including but not limited to: business strategies, financials, customer data, marketing performance data, internal tools, supplier relationships, product roadmaps, and personal data of other Fellows and employees.

4.2 The Fellow agrees to:
- Treat all such information as **strictly confidential**
- Not disclose, copy, publish, or use it for any purpose other than fulfilling Fellowship activities
- Not share it with any third party, including family, friends, or other employers
- Return or securely delete all confidential materials upon termination of the Fellowship

4.3 These confidentiality obligations **survive the termination** of this Agreement for **five (5) years**.

### 5. Intellectual Property

5.1 All work products created by the Fellow in the course of the Fellowship — including but not limited to documents, code, designs, campaigns, analyses, videos, images, and written content — **belong exclusively to Thiocyn GmbH** (or the respective HSB portfolio company, as directed).

5.2 The Fellow hereby assigns and transfers to Thiocyn all rights, title, and interest (including worldwide copyrights, trademark rights, and any other intellectual property rights) in such work products, to the full extent permitted by applicable law.

5.3 The Fellow waives any moral rights in the work products to the extent legally permissible.

5.4 For jurisdictions where such a blanket assignment is not automatically enforceable, the Fellow agrees to execute any additional documents reasonably necessary to effectuate this assignment, at Thiocyn's expense.

### 6. Data Protection (GDPR)

6.1 Thiocyn processes the Fellow's personal data (name, email, address, identification documents, emergency contact, etc.) for the purposes of administering the Fellowship, in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG).

6.2 The Fellow grants consent for Thiocyn to:
- Store this data in Business OS (hosted on Supabase EU infrastructure)
- Use it to communicate about the Fellowship, assign tasks, track progress, and issue references
- Retain it for the duration of the Fellowship plus 3 years for program-improvement analytics (anonymised where possible)

6.3 The Fellow has the right to request access, correction, deletion, or portability of their personal data at any time by contacting **hr@thiocyn.de** (or a designated contact).

6.4 If the Fellow's work involves processing personal data of customers (e.g. in Marketing or Support), they will receive separate GDPR training and must adhere to Thiocyn's data-processing policies.

### 7. Expenses and Reimbursements

7.1 The Fellowship is **unpaid**. No salary, stipend, or honorarium is provided.

7.2 Reasonable expenses **pre-approved in writing** by the Programme Lead (e.g. travel to an in-person event) may be reimbursed upon submission of receipts. The Fellow provides bank details only for this purpose if needed — not for any regular payment.

### 8. Duration and Termination

8.1 This Agreement begins on the Start Date stated in Section 1.2 and ends automatically after 12 weeks.

8.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement **with 7 calendar days' written notice** (email suffices), without stating a reason.

8.3 Thiocyn may terminate this Agreement **immediately and without notice** in case of:
- Breach of confidentiality (Section 4)
- Violation of data protection obligations (Section 6)
- Misconduct incompatible with continued participation (e.g. harassment, fraud)

8.4 Upon termination:
- The Fellow returns or deletes all confidential materials (Section 4.2)
- All IP-assignment obligations (Section 5) remain in force for work completed prior to termination
- Confidentiality obligations (Section 4.3) survive for 5 years

### 9. Conversion to Employment (Optional)

9.1 At the end of the Fellowship Period, Thiocyn may — at its sole discretion — offer the Fellow a **paid employment contract**. Such an offer is not guaranteed and is subject to a separate Graduation-Pitch assessment per the Assessment Rubrics.

9.2 If a paid contract is offered and accepted, it will supersede this Agreement and include standard German payroll setup (Steuer-ID, Sozialversicherung, Krankenversicherung).

### 10. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

10.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Federal Republic of Germany**, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles.

10.2 Exclusive jurisdiction for any dispute arising out of this Agreement lies with the courts of **Hamburg, Germany**, to the extent permitted by applicable mandatory consumer-protection or labor laws of the Fellow's country of residence.

10.3 If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force. The parties shall replace the invalid provision with a valid one that most closely reflects the original intent.

### 11. Entire Agreement

11.1 This Agreement constitutes the complete understanding between the parties concerning the Fellowship. Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

11.2 Side letters, verbal promises, or preliminary discussions are superseded by this Agreement.
