# Specialisation Tracks

In Month 2 each intern picks one of 5 tracks. The choice should align with both the intern's strengths and the company's current needs. Track leads supervise execution; the intern owns the deliverable.

For each track: scope, what the Big Target looks like, 2–3 example projects, success metrics, and the typical track lead.

---

## 1. Growth Marketing

**Track lead:** Matiz (CMO) or Valentin (Performance)
**Tools:** Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, Klaviyo, Shopify, GetKlar, GA4

**Big Target shape:** Design and execute one paid campaign or one CRO experiment, end-to-end, with measurable results.

**Scope:** Brief → creatives → setup → launch → analyse → recommend.

### Example projects

**A. Meta Ads creative test**
- Pick one brand and one audience
- Build 4 ad creatives across 2 angles
- Run 7-day test with €500 budget
- Deliverable: campaign brief, creatives, test results, recommendation
- Success metric: identified winning angle by CPA or ROAS

**B. Email flow build**
- Pick one high-traffic flow (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase)
- Design 4-email sequence with copy + design
- Set up in Klaviyo, A/B test subject lines
- Deliverable: flow live, results after 14 days
- Success metric: open rate >40%, attributed revenue tracked

**C. Landing page CRO test**
- Pick one product page underperforming on conversion
- Identify 2–3 hypotheses for the friction
- Build a variant + run A/B test
- Deliverable: variant page, test result, decision
- Success metric: statistically significant improvement OR clear "no" with learnings

## 2. Creative & Brand

**Track lead:** Brand Manager or Creative Director
**Tools:** Figma, CapCut, ChatGPT/Claude for copy, brand guidelines

**Big Target shape:** Produce one significant creative output that ships externally.

### Example projects

**A. Brand campaign concept**
- Pick a brand from House of Sustainable Brands
- Develop concept: insight → angle → executions
- Deliverable: 1-page concept doc + 3 sample executions (1 video script, 1 ad creative, 1 organic post)
- Success metric: concept approved by brand lead, at least 1 execution shipped

**B. UGC content sprint**
- Plan, brief, and produce 5 pieces of UGC content for one brand
- Deliverable: shot list, briefs sent, raw footage delivered, edits done
- Success metric: 5 finished pieces ready for paid testing

**C. Brand asset overhaul**
- Pick one underused brand surface (about page, packaging copy, email footer, etc.)
- Audit current state, propose new direction, ship the redesign
- Deliverable: before/after, reasoning doc
- Success metric: shipped, no regressions

## 3. Operations & Customer Support

**Track lead:** Sameer (CS Lead) or Ops Manager
**Tools:** Freshdesk/Zendesk, Shopify, Notion, Make/Zapier

**Big Target shape:** Improve one operational process so it costs less time, fewer errors, or scales better.

### Example projects

**A. CS workflow automation**
- Identify a repetitive CS task (refund processing, address changes, FAQ responses)
- Design automation (Make scenario, macro, template, etc.)
- Build, test, deploy
- Deliverable: automation live, before/after time saved
- Success metric: >2h/week saved, no error increase

**B. Returns process redesign**
- Audit current returns flow end-to-end
- Identify the 3 biggest bottlenecks
- Propose + ship 1 concrete improvement
- Deliverable: process map (before/after), implementation plan, week 1 results
- Success metric: faster processing OR fewer escalations

**C. CS knowledge base build**
- Take the top 20 CS tickets
- Write canonical answers + organise into searchable doc
- Roll out to team, measure usage
- Deliverable: KB live, team adoption tracked
- Success metric: 50%+ tickets resolved with KB reference

## How to choose a track

The intern submits a 1-paragraph rationale in Week 5. They should answer:

1. Why this track? (interest + strength)
2. Which of the example projects appeals most, and why?
3. What outcome would make Month 2 a success for them personally?

Track leads veto if:
- The track is over-subscribed and a better fit exists elsewhere
- The intern is reaching for something with no foundation (e.g. Finance track with no spreadsheet experience)
- Two interns picking the same project would create coordination overhead

---

## Track-lead responsibilities

Each track lead owns:
- 30-minute kickoff with the intern in Week 5
- Weekly 15-minute progress check in Weeks 6 and 7
- Final review + scoring in Week 8 presentation
- Honest feedback throughout — better to course-correct early than fail a presentation
