# Buddy Program

Every intern is paired with a full-time employee on Day 1. The buddy is **not a manager**. They are a trusted point of contact: someone the intern can ask "stupid questions" without judgement, get unstuck quickly, and learn how the company actually works through.

## Why this matters

Without structured peer contact, interns drift. They sit silently for days, accumulating small friction (where do I find X? who decides Y?), and either ask too late or don't ask at all. The buddy program kills that friction in the first 5 minutes of the working day.

## What the buddy provides

- **A name and a face** — someone to message on Slack without thinking about it
- **Operational shortcuts** — "the doc you need is in this folder" / "go ask Vanessa, she handles that"
- **Cultural translation** — how decisions actually get made vs. what the org chart says
- **Honest feedback** — what's working, what's not, low-stakes
- **A small task pipeline** — buddies can delegate simple tasks to interns, both benefit

## What the buddy does NOT do

- ❌ Performance reviews (that's Danylo + Luis)
- ❌ Decide on promotions, exits, or hiring
- ❌ Set the intern's main work priorities
- ❌ Replace the track lead

## Cadence (mandatory)

| When | What | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| **Week 1, Day 2** | Intro 1:1 — get to know each other, walk through company landscape | 30 min |
| **Weeks 2–4, every Tuesday** | Quick check-in 1:1 | 15 min |
| **Weeks 5–8, every other Tuesday** | Check-in 1:1 | 20 min |
| **Weeks 9–12, weekly** | Check-in 1:1 — increasingly intern-led | 15 min |

Slack contact in between is encouraged. The intern is expected to take initiative — buddies confirm, suggest, and unblock, but don't chase.

---

## Conversation starters

Every check-in needs structure or it becomes "everything's fine" small-talk. The intern should pick 1–2 of these per meeting:

### Week 1 (intro session)
- What's your role here, and how did you get into it?
- What's one thing you wish you'd known on your first day?
- What's a question I shouldn't be afraid to ask anyone?
- Who else should I meet in the first 2 weeks?

### Weeks 2–4 (Foundation phase)
- I worked on X this week. What would a senior person notice that I'd miss?
- I got stuck on Y. How would you have approached it?
- I'm trying to understand how Z works in this company. Can you walk me through it?
- What's one thing I should be doing differently?

### Weeks 5–8 (Specialisation phase)
- Here's my Big Target. What's the biggest risk you see?
- I made decision X this week. Would you have made the same call?
- What does "good" look like for this kind of project?
- Where do I underestimate the difficulty?

### Weeks 9–12 (Ownership phase)
- Here are my function metrics. What story do they tell you?
- I'm planning to do X next week. Talk me out of it or in to it.
- What's one habit I should keep after this internship?
- If you were me, what would you spend the last 3 weeks proving?

## Buddy → admin loop

After every 1:1, the buddy spends 60 seconds writing a 2-line note in the academy view (or via Slack if the integration isn't there yet):
1. **Status:** Green / Yellow / Red
2. **One observation:** something specific (not "going well")

Yellow or Red triggers a Danylo check-in within 48h. Most issues caught at Yellow are fixable.

---

## Choosing buddies

Good buddies have:
- 6+ months at the company (so they know the ropes)
- Time on Slack during business hours
- Patience for "obvious" questions
- A bias toward saying yes to a 15-minute meeting

Bad fits:
- Currently in a high-stress phase of their own work
- Default to "figure it out yourself"
- Don't use Slack regularly
- Already overcommitted to mentoring others

Danylo assigns buddies in Week 1 of each cohort. Reassignment is possible (and not a failure) if the chemistry is off — it's more important to find the right pairing than to preserve the original assignment.

---

## What buddies get out of it

- **Help with their own backlog** — interns can take simple tasks (data entry, research, draft writing, screenshot collecting)
- **Mentoring practice** — useful for anyone considering a team lead role
- **Fresh perspective** — interns notice things insiders have stopped seeing
- **Recognition** — being a good buddy is publicly noted in performance reviews

The deal is: 30 minutes/week of structured time, in exchange for a free pair of hands and a learning relationship.
