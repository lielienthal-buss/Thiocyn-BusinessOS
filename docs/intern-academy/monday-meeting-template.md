# Monday Meeting Template

The Monday meeting is the backbone of the Hartlimes Intern Academy. It runs every Monday at a fixed time (suggested: 10:30) and creates a weekly rhythm of accountability, visibility, and learning.

Every meeting follows the same 3-part structure. The middle section (Core) varies by week.

---

## Cadence by week

| Week | Format | Core focus |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1    | Themed | Welcome, programme overview, intern intros |
| 2–3  | Themed | Operations deep dive, brand walk-throughs |
| 4    | **Presentation** | Month 1 Assessment — Consultancy Plan Pitches |
| 5    | Themed | Track selection rationale, Big Target briefs |
| 6–7  | Themed | Track-specific deep dives, business case discussions |
| 8    | **Presentation** | Month 2 Assessment — Big Target Pitches |
| 9    | Themed | Function ownership briefings, KPI definition |
| 10–11| Themed | Open ideation, peer reviews, leadership Q&A |
| 12   | **Presentation** | Graduation Project — Final Presentations |

---

## Standard 3-part structure (60 minutes)

### Part 1 — Alignment & Activation (15 min)

**Goal:** Create energy and shared awareness. Every intern speaks.

Format:
- Optional 2-minute icebreaker (rotating prompt)
- Each intern: 30–60 second standup
  - What I shipped last week (1 thing)
  - My focus this week (1 thing)
  - One blocker / challenge

Tactics to keep engagement up:
- Rotate the speaking order each week
- Sometimes prompt-based: "Biggest win" / "Most surprising thing you learned"
- Cold-call lightly if needed — never punitively

---

### Part 2 — Core Segment (30 min)

#### Format A — Presentation Week

For Weeks 4, 8, and 12.

- 1–3 interns present (10 min each + 3 min Q&A)
- Scoring panel: Luis, Peter, Tom (or relevant track lead)
- Use the relevant rubric (Month 1, Month 2, or Graduation)
- Capture scores in the Business OS academy view
- After each presentation: 1 specific compliment + 1 specific improvement from each panellist

#### Format B — Themed Session

For all other weeks. Pick ONE topic. Never run unstructured.

Theme options (rotate):
1. **Business case discussion** — pick a real campaign / decision / outcome from the past week, dissect it together
2. **Process improvement brainstorm** — one workflow, propose changes
3. **Operations deep dive** — walk-through of a part of the business (CS, Ads, Logistics, etc.)
4. **Brand spotlight** — focus on one of the 6 brands, intern leads
5. **Personal development topic** — productivity, career direction, learning method
6. **Open ideation** — interns submit ideas in advance, group prioritises
7. **Guest speaker / external perspective** — invite a colleague or external voice

Themed sessions can be **led by interns** as a quest — assign one intern per week to prepare and run the core 30 minutes. Builds presentation muscle.

---

### Part 3 — Feedback, Recognition & Open Floor (15 min)

- 1 group recognition: who did something exceptional this week, named publicly
- Top-down feedback: leadership shares 1 insight about the business or programme
- Open Q&A: interns can ask anything (max 2–3 questions)
- Close with: next week's focus + reminder of upcoming deadlines

---

## Pre-meeting checklist (Sunday evening)

- [ ] Confirm meeting room / Zoom link
- [ ] Confirm format for the week (presentation vs themed)
- [ ] If themed: confirm topic + who's leading
- [ ] If presentation: confirm presenters + send rubric reminders
- [ ] Send agenda preview to intern Slack channel by Sunday 20:00
- [ ] Block 15 minutes after the meeting for notes + action items

---

## Post-meeting actions

- [ ] Log meeting in `monday_meetings` table (date, format, topic, attendees)
- [ ] Capture attendance + presentation scores in `intern_meeting_attendance`
- [ ] Note any flagged action items in the relevant intern's profile
- [ ] Send recap to intern channel within 24h: 3 bullets — what was covered, key takeaway, next week's focus

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- ❌ Running over time (60 min hard cap)
- ❌ Letting one person monopolise the standup
- ❌ Themed sessions without a clear topic ("just chat")
- ❌ Presentations without scoring or feedback
- ❌ Monologue from leadership in Part 3 — keep it interactive
- ❌ Skipping recognition — it costs nothing and matters disproportionately
